AWS Auto Scaling offers three main strategies to manage your EC2 instances: **Manual**, **Scheduled**, and **Dynamic Scaling**. Each serves a different purpose depending on your application's needs.

**What it is**: You manually adjust the number of EC2 instances in your Auto Scaling group.

**Use case**: Useful for testing or when you want full control over scaling.

**How to set up**:

- Go to the **EC2 Auto Scaling Groups** section in the AWS Console.
- Select your Auto Scaling group.
- Click **Edit** and change the **Desired Capacity**, **Min**, or **Max** values.
- Save changes‚ÄîAWS will launch or terminate instances to match the new desired capacity.

### ‚è∞ 2. Scheduled Scaling

**What it is**: You define scaling actions to occur at specific times (e.g., scale up every Monday at 9 AM).
**Use case**: Ideal for predictable workloads like business hours or seasonal traffic.
**How to set up**:
- In the EC2 Auto Scaling console, go to your Auto Scaling group.
- Under **Scheduled Actions**, click **Create Scheduled Action**.
- Set:
    - **Start time** (must be in the future)
    - **Recurrence** (optional, for repeated actions)
    - **Desired capacity**, **Min**, and **Max**
- Save the action. AWS will scale your group at the specified time.

### ‚öôÔ∏è 3. [Dynamic Scaling](obsidian://open?vault=Obsidian%20Vault&file=EC2%2F3.%20Auto%20Scaling%20Group%2F4.%20Dynamic%20scaling)
**What it is**: Automatically adjusts capacity based on real-time metrics like CPU usage or request count.

**Use case**: Best for applications with unpredictable or fluctuating traffic.

**How to set up**:

- Go to your Auto Scaling group in the AWS Console.
    
- Under **Automatic Scaling**, choose **Add Policy**.
    
- Select a policy type:
    
    - **Target Tracking**: Keeps a metric (e.g., CPU usage) at a target value.
        
    - **Step Scaling**: Scales based on how much a metric breaches a threshold.
        
    - **Simple Scaling**: Adds/removes a fixed number of instances when a CloudWatch alarm triggers.
        
- Define the metric, thresholds, and scaling adjustments.
    
- Save the policy‚ÄîAWS will monitor and act based on your configuration.
